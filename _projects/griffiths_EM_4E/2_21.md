## Part A

To find the potential outside the sphere with the given information, we start by finding the electric field there. Luckily, <a href="https://coltonkawamura.github.io/coltonkawamura/projects/griffiths_EM_4E/2_11/">we've done this before</a>.

To get the potential using this field, we compute a line integral of this field as we walk from infinity to the the point $r$ were we want to evaluate the potential

$$
\begin{align*}
V_\text{inside}&= -\int_0^{x'} \mathbf{E}_\text{inside} \cdot d \mathbf{r} \\
&= -\int_0^{x'} \left. (ky^2)\hat{x}\right|_{y=z=0} \cdot dx \hat{x} \\
&=0. \label{1} \tag{1}
\end{align*}
$$
