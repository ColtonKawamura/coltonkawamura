## Part A
We're hinted at using our results from <a href="https://coltonkawamura.github.io/coltonkawamura/projects/griffiths_EM_4E/2_7/">Problem 2.7</a> to start with this problem,


$$\begin{align*} 
\mathbf{E}_z&=\frac{R^{2}\sigma}{2\epsilon_{0}z^{2}}\left( \frac{(z-R)}{|z-R|}-\frac{(-z-R)}{|z+R|}\right) \hat{z} \tag{1} \label{1}.
\end{align*}$$

Equation \ref{1} is the vertical component for the electric field of a spherical shell. What we have in the problem, is a solid sphere, but in physics, that's just a fancy way of saying a bunch of concentric spherical shells.
