## Part A

So we start with the general form of electric field at a point $$r$$ away,

$$\begin{align*} 
\mathbf{E}=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}\frac{q}{r^2}\hat{x}. \tag{1} \label{1}
\end{align*}$$

This is a continuous charge distribution, so our $$q=\lambda / L$$. We're going to need to calculate the electric field from one end of the line segment to the other. When we do this $$r$$ varies. Let's get $$r$$ in a form that includes out given distances. We establish our $$x$$-axis as show below.

$$\begin{align*} 
r=\sqrt{d^2+x^2}. \tag{2} \label{2}
\end{align*}$$
