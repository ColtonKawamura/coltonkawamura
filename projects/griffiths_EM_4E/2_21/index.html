Part A

To find the potential outside the sphere with the given information, we start by finding the electric field there. Luckily, we’ve done this before.

To get the potential using this field, we compute a line integral of this field as we walk from infinity to the the point \(r'\) were we want to evaluate the potential,

\[\begin{align*} V_\text{outside}&= -\int_\infty^{r'} \mathbf{E}_\text{outside} \cdot d \mathbf{r} \\ &= -\int_\infty^{r'} \left( \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{q}{r^2} \right)\hat{r} \cdot dr \hat{r} \\ &=\left.\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} Q \left( \frac{1}{r} \right)\right|_{\infty}^{r'} \\ &= {\frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}}{\frac{1}{r}}.\label{1} \tag{1} \end{align*}\]

I dropped the \('\) off the \(r'\) for simplicity in equation \ref{1}.

Part B

Calculating the potential inside is a little more involved. We have walk our line integral from \(r=\infty\) to the surface of the sphere. During this path, \(\mathbf{E}=\mathbf{E}_\text{outside}\). But then we experience a discontinuity when we walk into the inside of the sphere, where \(\mathbf{E}=\mathbf{E}_\text{inside}\).

*PHOTO

Our line integral needs to be broken up to account for this,

\[\begin{align*} V_\text{inside}&= -\int_\infty^{R} \mathbf{E}_\text{outside} \cdot d \mathbf{r} +-\int_R^{r'} \mathbf{E}_\text{inside} \cdot d \mathbf{r}\\ &= {\frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}}{\frac{1}{R}}-\int_R^{r'} \left( \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{rq}{R^2} \right)\hat{r} \cdot dr \hat{r} \\ &={\frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}}{\frac{1}{R}}-\frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}{\frac{1}{R^{3}}}\left({\frac{r^{2}{-}R^{2}}{2}}\right) \\ &= {\frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}}{\frac{1}{r}}.\label{2} \tag{2} \end{align*}\]
