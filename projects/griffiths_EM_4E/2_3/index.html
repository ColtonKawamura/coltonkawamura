Part A

We’re dealing with a charge distribution here, instead of a single point charge. That means each little chuck of charge \(dq\) is going to contribute a little to the overall electric field.

What we want to do is add up all the little contributions to the electric field from each chuck. We can due this by writing Coulomb’s law in integral form and integrating from one end \(x=0\) to the other \(x=L\),

\[\begin{align*} \mathbf{E}=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}} \int_0^L \frac{dq}{r^2}\hat{r}.\tag{1} \label{1} \end{align*}\]

Let’s do something about the \(\hat{r}\) and \(r\). From inspection of the diagram below, we should expect the electric field to have some \(x\) and \(z\) component.

Thus, we have

\[\begin{align*} r=\sqrt{x^2+z^2} \end{align*}\]

and

\[\begin{align*} r\hat{r}=z\hat{z}-x\hat{x}, \\ \hat{r}=\frac{z\hat{z}-x\hat{x}}{r}. \end{align*}\]

We make these substitutions in equation \ref{1},

\[\begin{align*} \mathbf{E}&=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}} \int_0^L \frac{dq}{\sqrt{x^2+z^2}^2}\frac{z\hat{z}-x\hat{x}}{\sqrt{x^2+z^2}} \\ &=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}} \int_0^L \frac{dq}{\left({x^2+z^2}\right)^{3/2}}\left({z\hat{z}-x\hat{x}}\right).\tag{2} \label{2} \end{align*}\]

Before we calculate the integral we make one last substitution. The charge is spread out evenly over the length of the line segment, thus \(dq=\lambda dx\). Equation \ref{2} becomes

\[\begin{align*} \mathbf{E}&=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}} \int_0^L \frac{\lambda dx}{\left({x^2+z^2}\right)^{3/2}}\left({z\hat{z}-x\hat{x}}\right)\\ &=\frac{\lambda}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}} \left(\int_0^L \frac{z\hat{z}}{\left({x^2+z^2}\right)^{3/2}}dx - \int_0^L \frac{x\hat{x}}{\left({x^2+z^2} \right)^{3/2}} dx\right) \\ &=\frac{\lambda}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}\left(\frac{1}{z}\frac{L}{\sqrt{z^{2}+L^{2}}}\hat{z}-\left[{\frac{1}{z}}\,-\,{\frac{1}{\sqrt{z^{2}+L^{2}}}}\right]\hat{x}\right). \tag{3} \label{3} \end{align*}\]

When \(z\gg L\), the line segment will just look like a point charge with total charge \(q\). Let’s make sure math backs this up. With \(z\gg L\) equation \ref{3} becomes,

\[\begin{align*} \mathbf{E} &=\frac{\lambda}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}\left(\frac{1}{z}\frac{L}{\sqrt{z^{2}+0^{2}}}\hat{z}-\left[{\frac{1}{z}}\,-\,{\frac{1}{\sqrt{z^{2}+0^{2}}}}\right]\hat{x}\right) \\ &=\frac{\lambda}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}\left(\frac{L}{z^2}\hat{z}-\left[{\frac{1}{z}}\,-\,{\frac{1}{z}}\right]\hat{x}\right)\\ &=\frac{\lambda}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}\frac{L}{z^2}\hat{z}. \end{align*}\]

And \(q=\lambda L\), so,

\[\begin{align*} \mathbf{E} &=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}\frac{q}{z^2}\hat{z}. \end{align*}\]
